Framing the Work for Kore Marketing Agency.

At A Glance: A brand refresh and website redesign to help Kore Marketing present a more modern, credible presence and convert more prospects into conversations. The work focused on clarity, consistency, and a flexible system that could scale as the agency grew.

Role: Designer And Developer (Brand Identity + Web Experience).

Partners: Client Stakeholders And A Small Delivery Team.

Scope: Logo + Identity System, Print Collateral, Responsive Website, And Lead Capture Flows.

Tools: Illustrator, Photoshop, HTML/CSS, WordPress, And Motion Assets (As Needed).

Constraints.
  • Speed And Budget: deliver a high-impact refresh without overbuilding.
  • Flexibility: the identity needed to work across print, web, and social with minimal ongoing overhead.
  • Conversion: the site had to clearly explain services and drive inquiries quickly.
What I Owned.
  • Logo concepting and a responsive mark system for real-world usage.
  • Palette, typography, and layout patterns to keep the brand consistent across touchpoints.
  • Website structure, page layouts, and messaging integration for services and positioning.
  • Front-end build and WordPress implementation for maintainability and future updates.
  • Print and stationery assets aligned to the identity system.
Outcomes.
  • A cohesive identity system that scaled from signage down to a favicon.
  • A modern website that clearly communicated services and encouraged inbound leads.
  • Reusable page patterns and forms that supported future growth and iteration.

The Mark.

The Kore mark blends a tech-forward feel with the binary 1 and 0. The raised “O” acts as a subtle signal of elevated quality, while a streamlined palette supports a clean, modern presence across digital and print applications.

A Responsive Brand.

The logo system was designed to be responsive from day one. Larger applications use the full lockup with the by-line. Most everyday use relies on the primary full mark. For smaller footprints, the distilled 1 & 0 element works well for accents, backgrounds, and social profiles. At the smallest sizes, an ultra-simplified mark supports favicon-level clarity.

Print & Stationery.

To accompany the new brand roll out new business cards, print collateral, and advertisements were implemented.

The Website.

The website was designed as a clear marketing hub. It explains Kore’s services, reinforces the brand promise, and moves visitors toward contacting the team. The structure prioritized quick scanning, strong hierarchy, and lead capture moments placed where users naturally look for next steps.

Detailed overview information of service offerings

A steady dose of contact forms and links to bring in leads

Tailored messaging used throughout the site

Built on WordPress for future expandability

Result: A cohesive, modern presence that made the agency’s offering easier to understand, and made it easier for prospects to reach out.

Homepage Pattern.

The focus of the Kore Marketing homepage was to deliver a message on how they differ from other agencies in that they don't just produce work but they want to be your partner, friend, and succeed with you.

We offer an overview run down of the company's offerings with opportunities to drill in for more information along with a contact form in the footer to work on conversions and outreach right away.

About & Contact.

I designed and built out internal pages detailing more about Kore's values and advantages while keeping a bit of personality. Throughout the site contact information and forms are sprinkled throughout for lead conversion.

Services & Capabilities.

Each of the main services that Kore provides is given a page with an outlined description of what is offered and what is delivered. Each service page also includes an FAQ to help answer potential client questions upfront.
